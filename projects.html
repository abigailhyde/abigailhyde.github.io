Temples Timeline

The Temples Timeline app was featured in the October 2021 edition of BYU Studies Quarterly. The app is available on the Google Play Store and Apple App Store. Temples Timeline displays a chronology of temples built by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints from 1836 to the present day in a spiral layout, suitable for handheld touchscreen devices. I worked on this with Tsz Chin Lam, Litian Zhang, Grace Xie, and our professor Dr. Geoff Draper.

BYUH Timeline

The BYUH Timeline app is a work in progress. It makes use of a similar spiral layout to the Temples Timeline app, this time displaying a history of BYU-Hawaii and the surrounding community of Laie. The timeline is available here. The first half of 2021 was spent procuring images for the app, the majority of them coming from the BYU-Hawaii archives. I worked on this with Tsz Chin Lam, Litian Zhang, and our professor Dr. Geoff Draper.

Speakeasy

Speakeasy was written for my CS 401 Web Apps Development class at BYU-Hawaii in Fall 2022. Inspired by the now defunct Lang-8 website, Speakeasy allows users to learn language and culture in a fun, social way.

Henriette

"Henriette" was my final project for CS 490R at BYU-Hawaii in Fall 2021. "Henriette" tells the story of a mother hen who has lost her chicks. I developed the game over a one month span using Unity. It can be played here, and source code is available on GitHub along with a downloadable build.

"Sunburst Chart: Cremation vs. Burial and Other Disposition Techniques"

"Sunburst Chart: Cremation vs. Burial and Other Disposition Techniques" was my final project for CS 490R at BYU-Hawaii in Winter 2022. This visualization displays cremation statistics for five countries, making it easy to compare trends over time. The dataset and code can be found here. This program was written in Java, and made extensive use of JDBC.
